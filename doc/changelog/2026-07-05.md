# Changelog - 2026-07-05

## Summary
Editor follow-up on floor resizing: changing the warehouse width/depth now recenters the camera on the whole new floor (the extra depth grows toward the viewer and was off-screen, making the new space unreachable by drag), with a floor-proportional framing that keeps every corner visible. The front/back corridors — which bound aisle movement and were invisible (reported as "objects can't move along the whole y axis") — are now drawn on the floor as translucent circulation bands.

## Changes

- [web/public/js/main.js] Changing the floor width/depth in the global fields recenters the camera (`applyWorkingDef` forwards a `recenter` flag); all other edits still keep the user's viewpoint
- [web/public/js/scene.js] Recenter framing scales with the floor (camera at width/2 + span·0.5, span·1.05, depth/2 + span·0.95 with span = max dimension): the whole floor, near edge included, fits on screen after a resize — the previous framing cut the near edge for deep floors; front/back corridors rendered as translucent floor-marking grey bands (distinct from the receiving zone's steel blue), non-pickable, rebuilt with the statics so they follow the Couloir avant/arrière fields live, each labelled « Couloir avant » / « Couloir arrière » (labels placed right of the racks where nothing overlaps in the default view)
- [web/public/js/layout.js] Added `corridorBands` (pure): front/back corridor strips spanning the full floor width, with French labels
- [tests/unit/web/layout.test.js] corridorBands positions, extent and labels
- [tests/ui/editeur.spec.js] New test: resizing the floor depth (computed from the disposable warehouse, not hardcoded) recenters the camera on the new floor centre while other edits leave it untouched (existing test)

## Notes
Verified end-to-end in a real browser on a disposable copy: with depth 42 → 62, the far corners and the near edge now project inside the canvas, and a real pointer drag brings the Réception zone to the very edge of the floor (front edge y = 59, back edge = 62 = floor boundary). The default view of the example warehouse improves as a side effect (the Réception zone was previously cut off at the bottom). The remaining "can't move along the whole y axis" symptom was the back corridor doing its job invisibly: aisles must stay between the corridors, which are now visible on the floor. 164 unit tests and 13 UI tests pass.
