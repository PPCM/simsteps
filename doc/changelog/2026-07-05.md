# Changelog - 2026-07-05

## Summary
Display option: a « Masquer les libellés » switch in the Piloter tab hides all 3D label sprites (aisles, zones, corridors); while hidden, clicking an element — corridor bands included — reveals its own label (click in the void hides it again).

Editor follow-up on floor resizing: changing the warehouse width/depth now recenters the camera on the whole new floor (the extra depth grows toward the viewer and was off-screen, making the new space unreachable by drag), with a floor-proportional framing that keeps every corner visible. The front/back corridors — which bound aisle movement and were invisible (reported as "objects can't move along the whole y axis") — are now drawn on the floor as translucent circulation bands.

## Changes

- [web/public/js/main.js] Changing the floor width/depth in the global fields recenters the camera (`applyWorkingDef` forwards a `recenter` flag); all other edits still keep the user's viewpoint; « Masquer les libellés » switch wired to the scene, with click-to-reveal on the canvas (orbit-end clicks filtered out by pointer travel) and `labelStats` added to the `window.simstepsDebug` test handle
- [web/public/js/scene.js] Recenter framing scales with the floor (camera at width/2 + span·0.5, span·1.05, depth/2 + span·0.95 with span = max dimension): the whole floor, near edge included, fits on screen after a resize — the previous framing cut the near edge for deep floors; front/back corridors rendered as translucent floor-marking grey bands (distinct from the receiving zone's steel blue), non-pickable, rebuilt with the statics so they follow the Couloir avant/arrière fields live, each labelled « Couloir avant » / « Couloir arrière » (labels placed right of the racks where nothing overlaps in the default view); label sprites tracked with their element (`setLabelsVisible`, `revealLabel`, `labelStats`, `pick`) so the global switch and the click-to-reveal survive scene rebuilds; corridor bands wrapped in `{type: 'corridor'}` groups, pickable like any element: click reveals their label, and in edit mode they are selectable (panel shows « Couloir avant/arrière » with a Position (y) field synced with the global fields) and draggable along y via `moveCorridor` (metre snap, clamped between the floor edge and the aisles' outlet margin); deleting a corridor is refused with a French message
- [web/public/js/layout.js] Added `corridorBands` (pure): front/back corridor strips spanning the full floor width, with French labels
- [tests/unit/web/layout.test.js] corridorBands positions, extent and labels
- [tests/ui/editeur.spec.js] New test: resizing the floor depth (computed from the disposable warehouse, not hardcoded) recenters the camera on the new floor centre while other edits leave it untouched (existing test)
- [web/public/index.html] « Masquer les libellés (clic : révéler) » switch in the Affichage section
- [web/public/js/editor/model.js] Added `moveCorridor` (pure): metre-snapped corridor move bounded by the floor edge (1 m), the aisles' outlet (CORRIDOR_MARGIN) and the other corridor
- [web/public/js/editor/panel.js] Corridor selection panel: French title and a single Position (y) field
- [tests/unit/web/editor-model.test.js] moveCorridor clamps/snap/non-mutation and validity of the moved definition
- [tests/ui/editeur.spec.js] New test: click-select the front corridor band (camera-projected point), drag it toward the floor edge — integer position, global field synced, no validation error
- [tests/ui/interface.spec.js] New test: hide labels, reveal one by clicking a rack (screen position computed by camera projection, independent of warehouse dimensions), hide it with a void click, reveal a corridor's label by clicking its band, restore all by unchecking

## Notes
Verified end-to-end in a real browser on a disposable copy: with depth 42 → 62, the far corners and the near edge now project inside the canvas, and a real pointer drag brings the Réception zone to the very edge of the floor (front edge y = 59, back edge = 62 = floor boundary). The default view of the example warehouse improves as a side effect (the Réception zone was previously cut off at the bottom). The remaining "can't move along the whole y axis" symptom was the back corridor doing its job invisibly: aisles must stay between the corridors, which are now visible on the floor. 164 unit tests and 13 UI tests pass.
